## Overview
<p align="justify">
This section introduces the design principles and models used in enterprise campus networks. The enterprise campus architecture provides a scalable, modular, and resilient framework for large organizations. It emphasizes hierarchical design, redundancy, and segmentation to ensure performance, manageability, and security.
</p>
---

## Topics Covered
- Hierarchical network design model
  - Core layer
  - Distribution layer
  - Access layer
- Modular design principles
- Redundancy and high availability
- Scalability and performance optimization
- Security considerations in campus networks
- Quality of Service (QoS) in enterprise environments
- Best practices for enterprise campus design

---

## Notes & Diagrams
- **Hierarchical Model**:  
  - **Core Layer**: High-speed backbone, optimized for fast transport.  
  - **Distribution Layer**: Policy enforcement, routing, and redundancy.  
  - **Access Layer**: End-user device connectivity, VLAN segmentation.  

- **Design Principle**:  
  - Modularity allows networks to grow without redesigning the entire infrastructure.  

- **Diagram Idea**:  
  - Show a three-tier enterprise campus network with core, distribution, and access layers.
